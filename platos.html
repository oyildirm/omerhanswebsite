Plato's Cave Response

Plato's allegory of the cave was a concepted created by the philosopher to think deeply on how the truth and reality of a story can be shapened by what little knowledge a person can have. The prisoners inside of the cave who only know the image of darkness are presented with shadows of objects or puppets to give them something to imagine what the outside world is like, while living in a world of captivity for their whole lives. As one prisoner is set free he sees the true world that has been hidden from him, so he tries to save his fellow prisoners but to no avail. The other prisoners would listen to how their would be a new world out there but they are afraid of what is out there and have grown accustmed to their current situation. This concept can be seen in many pieces of fillms, the best example being the Truman Show.
